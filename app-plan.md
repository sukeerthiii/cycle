# Cycle — Phase-Aware Fitness Log for Women

## What it does

A cycle tracker at its core, with a workout log built into it. You log your period start/end. The app infers which of 4 phases you're in, tells you what to do AND when to rest, and logs everything with full detail. Over time, it shows you progression mapped to your cycle — and it has opinions about what "progress" actually means for a woman's body doing body recomposition.

## Philosophy — what this app believes

This section is the app's soul. Every suggestion, metric, and nudge flows from these beliefs.

### Body recomposition, not weight loss

The goal is to lose fat while building muscle. The scale might not move — that's success, not failure. The app never mentions scale weight. It measures progress through strength progression, cycle compliance, and how your body responds to the training across phases.

### Heavy > light for "toned"

"Toning" is a myth word for "build muscle + lose fat." The research is clear: heavy compound lifts (6-10 rep range) are more effective for body recomposition than light weight / high reps. Heavy lifting:

- Preserves muscle during fat loss
- Creates greater metabolic afterburn (EPOC)
- Drives meaningful body composition change

The app's suggestions reflect this. It will never suggest "light weights for toning." However, the user is a moderate lifter still building up — the app starts with a 10-12 rep range at manageable weights and narrows toward 6-10 as loads increase over cycles.

### Rest is the plan, not the absence of the plan

Muscle protein synthesis peaks 36-48 hours AFTER training. No rest = no growth. The app actively prescribes rest days as first-class suggestions. "Rest today. Your muscles are still building from yesterday." is not a consolation message — it's the most important suggestion the app can give.

Overtraining elevates cortisol. Cortisol makes stubborn fat MORE stubborn, especially in the midsection. Training 7 days straight is not consistency — it's self-sabotage. The app knows this and says so.

### Streaks are the wrong metric

Consecutive-day training streaks reward overtraining. The app does not track them. Instead, it tracks **cycle compliance**: did you follow phase-appropriate suggestions this cycle? Did you push when your body was ready (follicular/ovulatory)? Did you rest when your body needed it (menstrual)? Did you back off when progesterone said to (luteal)?

Compliance with the rhythm > raw frequency.

### Walks are first-class

Walking burns fat without spiking cortisol. 8,000+ steps daily alongside 3-4 strength sessions is the evidence-based protocol. The app treats walks with the same respect as heavy lifting — different tool, same importance.

### Your cycle IS your periodization

Traditional lifting programs use arbitrary 4-week blocks for periodization (push, deload, push, deload). Your body already has one built in. The app aligns training to your actual hormonal rhythm:

- **Follicular + Ovulatory**: anabolic window. Push progressive overload, heavy compounds, chase PRs.
- **Luteal**: maintain loads, don't chase new maxes. Volume can stay but intensity backs off.
- **Menstrual**: natural deload. Rest, walks, mobility/stretches. This is where the previous cycle's work consolidates.

### Optimal protocol for the stated goal

- 3-4 strength sessions per week (each muscle group 2x/week)
- Compound lifts: squat, deadlift, hip thrust, RDL, bench press, overhead press, rows, lunges
- Start at 10-12 rep range, narrow to 6-10 as loads increase over time. 3-4 sets.
- Progressive overload in follicular/ovulatory (add 2.5-5lb when you hit all target reps)
- Maintain in luteal, deload in menstrual
- Daily walks (8,000+ steps)
- Pilates 1-2x per week (core, flexibility)
- Yoga on non-period days for recovery/mobility
- **Menstrual phase specifically**: rest, walks, mobility/stretches ONLY. No yoga, no pilates, no strength. This is your personal preference and the app respects it as the default.
- At least 2 full rest days per week

### Phase-aware nutrition nudges (no food tracking)

The app doesn't track meals. But it knows what your body needs at each phase and when, so it gives you timely nudges:

Nudges mention **Indian ingredients** by name — mix of veg and non-veg, things you'd actually have in your kitchen or can pick up easily. Not recipes, just ingredients to keep in mind while cooking.

**Follicular**:

- "Post-workout protein: paneer, eggs, chicken, moong dal, curd."
- "Carb tolerance is high — rice, roti, sweet potato all fuel your lifts well right now."
- "Soya chunks: 52g protein per 100g. Cheapest protein source you have."
- Protein target: 1.6g/kg/day minimum

**Ovulatory**:

- "Insulin sensitivity peaks. Good time for rice-heavy meals around training."
- "Post-workout: eggs, paneer, chicken. Your MPS response is strongest right now."
- "Chana (chickpeas): protein + fiber + iron. A good ovulatory staple."

**Luteal**:

- "Progesterone breaks protein down faster. Aim higher — closer to 2g/kg. Extra dal, extra curd."
- "Craving carbs is real. Poha, upma, idli — carb-heavy snack 3-4h before training helps."
- "Magnesium: pumpkin seeds, almonds, dark chocolate, palak."
- "Ragi (finger millet): calcium + iron + protein. Good luteal grain."
- "Hydrate more than usual — nimbu pani, coconut water, chaas."

**Menstrual**:

- "Iron: masoor dal, rajma, palak, beetroot, dates, jaggery."
- "Anti-inflammatory: haldi doodh, ginger, fish, methi seeds."
- "Protein still matters — don't drop below 1.6g/kg. Curd, dal, eggs."
- "Ghee: anti-inflammatory, calorie-dense, easy to add to anything."

**Post-workout (any phase)**:

- "Protein within 2 hours. [ingredient for the current phase]."

These show as a collapsible "Nutrition" card on the Cycle tab. Dismissible, not persistent.

### Science education — learning your body over time

The app teaches you why, not just what. Two mechanisms:

**Phase transition explainer**: When you enter a new phase (detected from your cycle day), a brief card appears prominently on the Cycle tab:

- Entering **Follicular**: "Estrogen is rising. It drives muscle protein synthesis — your muscles respond better to heavy load right now. Progesterone is low, which means less water retention and more stable energy. This is your building window."
- Entering **Ovulatory**: "Estrogen peaks. So does your strength, reaction time, and pain tolerance. Testosterone has a small mid-cycle bump too. This is when your body can handle the most. Use it."
- Entering **Luteal**: "Progesterone is climbing. It's catabolic — it breaks protein down faster than follicular phase. Your body temperature rises ~0.3-0.5C, which means you fatigue faster in cardio. Maintain your strength, don't chase new numbers."
- Entering **Menstrual**: "Both hormones drop. Your body is shedding the uterine lining and recovering. Energy, strength, and motivation are at their lowest — that's physiology, not weakness. Rest. Walk. Stretch. The next building window is coming."

These are dismissible but saved in a "Learn" section in Settings if you want to reread them.

**Daily micro-snippet ("Why" card)**: A one-line science fact tied to today's suggestion, shown as a collapsible card on the Cycle tab:

- "Estrogen increases tendon stiffness — your joints are more stable for heavy squats right now." (follicular)
- "Your metabolic rate is 5-10% higher in luteal. You're burning more at rest, but tiring faster in sessions." (luteal)
- "Iron stores drop during menstruation. Your body prioritizes recovery over output." (menstrual)
- "Cortisol spikes from overtraining cause visceral fat storage — rest days keep cortisol low." (rest day)
- "Muscle protein synthesis peaks 24-48h after a session. Right now, yesterday's squats are becoming muscle." (day after strength)

These rotate — you don't see the same one twice until you've cycled through all of them for that phase. Over multiple cycles, you build a complete mental model of your own physiology.

Hormones are named plainly: estrogen, progesterone, cortisol, testosterone. No euphemisms. The app trusts you to learn the vocabulary.

## Phase model (women's physiology)

The backbone of every suggestion and trend view:

- **Menstrual** (day 1 to ~period length): Lower energy. Rest, walks, mobility/stretches ONLY. No yoga, no pilates, no strength. Deload window.
- **Follicular** (post-period to ~day 13): Rising estrogen, energy climbing. Good window for progressive overload, heavier strength, intense pilates.
- **Ovulatory** (~day 14-16): Peak energy + power output. Heavy lifts, challenging flows, PR attempts.
- **Luteal** (~day 17 to cycle end): Progesterone up, energy tapering. Moderate strength (maintain, don't push PRs), restorative yoga, steady walks. Higher body temp — endurance may dip.

Phase boundaries are estimated from the user's inputted cycle length + period length, refined as more periods are logged. Cycle is very regular (within 1-2 days), so estimates are reliable.

### Edge cases

- **Irregular cycle (period comes early/late)**: Log the new period start whenever it happens. The app recalculates all phases from the new start date and adjusts future predictions. No questions asked.
- **Forgot to log period start**: Backdate support — when logging a period start, you can pick a past date ("Started on [date]") and the app recalculates retroactively.
- **Period end not logged**: The app assumes period length from your profile default. If you log the end date, it uses the actual date instead.
- **Skipped a day of logging entirely**: The app doesn't care. No empty-day guilt. The day simply has no entry. Compliance scoring only looks at days where something was expected (training days, prescribed rest days).

## Tech stack

- **React** + **TypeScript** — component-based, huge ecosystem, fast iteration
- **Vite** as the build tool (lighter than Next.js for a pure client-side app)
- **PWA** (Progressive Web App) — "Add to home screen" on iOS, full-screen, own icon, feels native
- **IndexedDB** (via Dexie.js) for local persistence — all data on-device, no backend
- **Framer Motion** for animations (spring physics, layout transitions, the phase ring)
- **CSS custom properties** for the design system (colors, typography, spacing)
- **EB Garamond** via Google Fonts
- **Recharts** or **Victory** for charts (phase bands, progression lines, heatmaps)
- **JSON/CSV export** via browser download
- **Deployed on Vercel** (free tier) — accessible from any device via URL
- No backend, no auth, no database server. Local-first, personal tool.
- **Monthly auto-backup**: the app prompts or auto-exports a JSON backup at the end of each cycle (when a new period is logged). Not just a manual button in settings.

## Data models

### UserProfile (hardcoded for v1 — this is a personal app)

- Age: 25
- Sex: Female
- Ethnicity: Indian
- Diet: mix of veg and non-veg
- Goal: body recomposition (lose stubborn fat, build lean muscle)
- Lifting experience: moderate (still building up to heavy)
- BMI: normal range
- Protein target: ~1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight (shifts by phase)
- Step target: 8,000/day
- Strength target: 3-4 sessions/week
- Unit preference: lb (weight), km (distance)

All suggestions, nudges, nutrition guidance, and compliance scoring are calibrated for this specific profile. Not generic. Not configurable in v1 — the app knows who it's for.

### CycleLog

- `periodStartDate: Date`
- `periodEndDate: Date?`
- `cycleLength: number` (user-set, adjustable)
- `periodLength: number`

### DailyLog

One per day. Multiple workout sessions in the same day merge into sections within this.

- `date: Date`
- `phase: Phase` (auto-derived from CycleLog)
- `sections: WorkoutSection[]`
- `steps: number | null` (manual daily step count, physically logged, entered end of day)
- `skipReason: string | null` (optional, if suggestion was skipped)
- `suggestedWorkout: SuggestedWorkout | null` (what the app suggested for this day — for compliance tracking)
- `plannedWorkout: PlannedWorkout | null` (if user pre-scheduled from the Movement tab)

### WorkoutSection

One per workout type logged that day. Morning strength + evening walk = two sections.

- `type: WorkoutType` (strength / pilates / yoga / mobility / walk)
- `exercises: ExerciseEntry[]` (for strength)
- `duration: number | null` (minutes, for pilates/yoga/walk)
- `steps: number | null` (for walk — auto-adds to DailyLog.steps)
- `distance: number | null` (for walk, in km)
- `notes: string | null`

### ExerciseEntry

- `exercise: Exercise`
- `sets: SetEntry[]`

### SetEntry

- `reps: number`
- `weight: number | null` (null = bodyweight, in lb)
- `isBodyweight: boolean`
- `duration: number | null` (seconds, for timed holds like planks)

### Exercise

- `name: string`
- `category: WorkoutType`
- `movementPattern: MovementPattern | null` (squat / hinge / lunge / push / pull / core — null for non-strength exercises)
- `isCustom: boolean`

## Logging — how you record a workout

### Entry point

Tap the FAB (from any tab). A bottom sheet slides up with five tappable cards. The suggested type for today is highlighted with a gold accent. Each card has a hand-drawn icon and the current phase color as a subtle tint.

Tapping the highlighted suggestion opens a **pre-filled log** (exercises, sets, reps, weight from history + phase-appropriate progression). Tapping any other type opens a blank log.

From the Movement tab, tapping today's date opens the day sheet where you can also tap the suggestion to trigger the pre-filled log.

### Strength flow

Logging happens **during the workout, between sets**. Speed is everything.

1. **Add exercise**: Search bar at top. Type to filter the built-in library + custom exercises. "Create custom" button if not found. Tap to add.
2. **"Last time" reference**: Immediately below the exercise name, a small muted card: "Last — 3x10 @ 110lb (follicular, Mar 12)". Always visible. You never have to remember.
3. **Set entry (quick-tap)**:
   - Each set is a row: `[Set 1]   10 reps   110 lb`
   - Pre-filled from last session's numbers (same weight + reps across sets, since that's your pattern)
   - Tap reps → inline +/- stepper (increments of 1)
   - Tap weight → inline +/- stepper (2.5 or 5 lb, per settings)
   - Weight field can show "BW" for bodyweight — tap to toggle between BW and a number
   - Swipe set row left → delete
   - "Add Set" below last row — copies reps/weight from the row above
4. **Add more exercises**: "Add Exercise" button below. Stacks vertically. Each exercise block is collapsible (tap name to collapse/expand) so the screen stays manageable.
5. **Save**: "Save Workout" at bottom. Optional notes field above it. Phase auto-tagged on save.

### Pilates / Yoga flow

Session-level only:

1. **Duration**: hour:minute picker or type a number
2. **Notes**: optional freeform (e.g. "reformer class" or "yin flow, hip openers")
3. **Save**

### Mobility / Stretches flow

Same as pilates/yoga:

1. **Duration**: picker or manual
2. **Notes**: optional freeform (e.g. "hip focus, foam roll, 90/90s")
3. **Save**

### Walk flow

1. **Duration**: picker or manual
2. **Steps**: optional numeric field. Steps logged here **auto-add to the daily step total** on the Cycle tab.
3. **Distance**: optional (km)
4. **Notes**: optional freeform
5. **Save**

### Daily merging

Multiple workouts in one day merge into **one daily entry** with separate sections. On the Movement tab and History, you see one card containing all sections. Each section has its own type icon and details.

### Editing past workouts

Tap any workout card (Movement tab day-sheet, or History tab) to reopen the log with all fields editable. Add sets, change weights, delete exercises, or delete the entire workout. No time limit on edits.

### After saving

- Workout appears in the Movement tab day-sheet and History
- "Last time" data updates for all exercises logged
- Walk steps auto-add to daily step counter on the Cycle tab
- Pattern checklist updates on the Movement tab
- Suggestion engine registers the session
- If it was the suggested workout, the suggestion in the day-sheet updates to "Logged" state

*Am I balanced across strength, pilates, yoga, walks?*

*Am I progressing from one cycle to the next?*

*How was my last cycle, holistically?*

Generated only at cycle end (when a new period is logged). No mid-cycle partial view.

  - Total workouts, volume, type split

  - Comparison to previous cycle (volume delta, frequency delta)
  - Flagged patterns: "You've ignored luteal deload suggestions 3 cycles in a row"
  - Positive callouts: "You hit 2 squat PRs this cycle, both in ovulatory"
- One card per completed cycle — stack grows as cycles accumulate. Most recent on top.

- **Add/remove**: "Edit dashboard" mode (pencil icon top-right) — toggle cards on/off, add duplicate exercise progression cards for different exercises
- **Time range**: Each expanded card has a time filter — "This cycle", "Last 3 cycles", "All time"

## Suggestions — how the app guides your training

### Where suggestions live

Suggestions show in two places:
- **FAB type picker**: the suggested type is highlighted with a gold accent. Tapping it opens a pre-filled log.
- **Movement tab day-sheet**: tapping today's date shows the suggestion as a summary card ("Strength — Lower Body, 4 exercises"). Tap to expand the full exercise list. Tap again to start the pre-filled log.

Every day, the app suggests ONE thing. It can be a workout OR rest. Both are equally valid.

**On a training day**, the suggestion includes:
- **Workout type + focus** (e.g. "Strength — Lower Body")
- **Intensity context** (e.g. "Follicular, day 8. Your body is ready to push.")
- **Summary view**: "Strength — Lower Body, 4 exercises" (tap to expand full list with sets/reps/weight)
- **Full exercise list** (expanded): uses last logged weight/reps, applies phase-appropriate progression.
- For new users with no history: just the workout TYPE + "log your weights — the app will learn from here."

**On a rest day**, the suggestion includes:
- "Rest today. Your muscles are still building from yesterday."
- Or menstrual: "Deload window. Your body is resetting. Walk, stretch, or do nothing."
- Or post-heavy session: "Recovery day. MPS is elevated for the next 36-48h — this is where the work becomes muscle."
- A walk suggestion: "A walk today keeps cortisol low and fat burning steady."

**Deload windows** are explicitly flagged:
- Late luteal: "Your energy is tapering. Drop volume ~30%, focus on movement quality."
- Menstrual: "Deload week. Walks, mobility, rest. No strength, no pilates, no yoga."

### Movement pattern framework — what you should be covering

For body recomp, 6 movement patterns matter. Each should be hit ~2x per week across 3-4 strength sessions:

- **Squat**: quads, glutes — back squat, goblet squat, leg press
- **Hinge**: posterior chain, glutes, hamstrings — deadlift, RDL, hip thrust, glute bridge
- **Lunge**: unilateral legs, stability — walking lunge, Bulgarian split squat, step-up
- **Push**: chest, shoulders, triceps — bench press, OHP, push-up, dumbbell press
- **Pull**: back, biceps — barbell row, lat pulldown, pull-up, face pull
- **Core**: stability, anti-rotation — plank, pallof press, dead bug, farmer's carry

Lower body patterns (squat, hinge, lunge) are the priority for your goal. Upper body (push, pull) is essential for metabolic rate. Core supports everything.

### Pattern coverage checklist

On the Movement tab, below the calendar: 6 circles in a row, one per pattern. Filled = hit this week. Hollow = missing. Resets weekly. Quick "what haven't I done yet?" without dictating your schedule.

### How the split works

The app doesn't lock you into a rigid split. You log whatever you actually do. The engine observes and fills gaps:

- Every exercise is auto-tagged with its pattern (squat, hinge, lunge, push, pull, core). Custom exercises auto-infer from name where possible, with manual override.
- If you've hit squat and lunge but no hinge → suggestion: "You haven't done a hinge movement this week — consider deadlifts or RDLs today."
- If you've done 3 lower sessions and no upper → suggestion shifts to push/pull.
- Auto-alternates upper/lower as the default rhythm, but never restricts.

The goal: every pattern hit ~2x per week, balanced across the cycle.

### Weekly rhythm

The app plans a weekly shape, not just one day at a time:
- 3-4 strength sessions (balanced across muscle groups)
- 1-2 pilates/yoga sessions (not during menstrual)
- Daily walk encouragement
- At least 2 prescribed rest days
- Adjusted per phase: more rest in luteal/menstrual, more intensity in follicular/ovulatory

### Pre-planned workouts

From the Movement tab, tapping a future day lets you pre-schedule an intention (e.g. "Lower body strength on Thursday"). When that day arrives, the suggestion engine uses your plan instead of generating one. If you don't pre-plan, the engine generates as usual.

### First cycle (no history)

The suggestion engine has no data yet. It suggests:
- Workout TYPE only (e.g. "Strength — Lower Body" or "Rest")
- No specific exercises or weights
- "Log your first session — the app learns from here."
- After 1-2 weeks of logging, suggestions become fully specific

### Progressive overload logic

- **Starting point**: 10-12 rep range at manageable weights (moderate lifter ramp-up)
- **Follicular + ovulatory**: If you hit all target reps last session, suggest +2.5-5lb or +1 rep. As loads increase, rep range narrows toward 6-10. This is where PRs live.
- **Luteal**: Maintain follicular loads. Same weight, same reps, possibly fewer sets.
- **Menstrual**: Rest, walks, mobility only. No lifting suggested.
- The cycle IS the periodization — no arbitrary 4-week blocks.

### Adaptive behavior

The suggestion engine learns from overrides silently:
- If you consistently do pilates when strength is suggested in luteal → suggests pilates in luteal after 2-3 cycles
- If you never do yoga in follicular → stops suggesting it
- Preferences are per-phase, not global
- Adaptation is silent and non-judgmental

The suggestion is a **starting point** — you'll often swap exercises or adjust. The UX makes overriding easy and natural, not like "rejecting" a plan.

### Skip logging

Optionally note why you skipped (cramps, travel, fatigue, not feeling it). Feeds into cycle report card. Not required.

### Compliance score

Tracks how well your training aligned with what your body needed:
- Did you push during follicular/ovulatory?
- Did you maintain (not chase PRs) during luteal?
- Did you rest during menstrual?
- Did you take your rest days?
- Did you hit your weekly session target (3-4, not 7)?

Shows as a percentage on the cycle report card and in the Trends stats row.

## Design system

### North star

Oura Ring's data clarity + warm grainy texture + a quiet Indian sensibility. Premium calm. It should feel like opening a beautiful personal journal that's deeply smart about your body. Not a gym app. Not a medical app. A personal artifact that happens to be Indian and happens to understand hormones.

Dark mode only. No light mode toggle.

### Color system

**Base surfaces**:

- Primary background: warm charcoal `#1C1917` (stone-900)
- Elevated surface (cards): `#292524` (stone-800) — separation through subtle shadow
- Deepest surface (sheets, modals): `#1A1614`

**Text**:

- Primary: warm off-white `#FAF5F0`
- Secondary: muted warm gray `#A8A29E` (stone-400)
- Tertiary/disabled: `#78716C` (stone-500)

**Phase colors**:

- Menstrual: deep terracotta `#C4705A` (Indian clay, mitti)
- Follicular: warm sage `#8BA888` (neem, fresh henna)
- Ovulatory: golden amber `#D4A843` (haldi, marigold)
- Luteal: dusty mauve `#9B7E9B` (jamun, dried lavender)

Each phase color has three intensities: full (rings, active accents), muted (~15% opacity for card backgrounds, calendar fills), glow (radial blur behind the phase ring).

**Accent**: warm gold `#D4A843` — same as ovulatory, doubles as CTA/FAB color.

### Typography

**EB Garamond** is the voice of the app. Scholarly, warm, editorial — nothing like any fitness app.

- **Display / hero numbers**: EB Garamond. Used for: cycle day inside the phase ring, PR numbers, stats row, report card headlines. 48pt for the hero number, 22pt for section headings.
- **Body / UI text**: System sans-serif (Inter or system default) for readability at smaller sizes — exercise names, set/rep labels, table cells, buttons. 15pt body, 13pt caption.
- **Numbers in tables**: Tabular figures (monospaced width) so columns align in the History tab.
- **Phase names**: EB Garamond, medium weight, tracked slightly wide. e.g. `F O L L I C U L A R`.

### Grain texture

Grain only in v1. Block-print motifs deferred to a polish pass.

**Grain**: Noticeable, like risograph or 35mm at ISO 800. Tileable PNG noise at ~8-12% opacity via CSS `mix-blend-mode: soft-light`. Warm-tinted. On data surfaces (charts, tables), dialed to ~5%.

### Icons

Custom hand-drawn, organic, slightly imperfect (pen-on-paper feel).

- **Phase icons** (secondary to color): abstract organic shapes. A drop (menstrual), a sprout (follicular), a sun (ovulatory), a leaf (luteal).
- **Workout type icons**: hand-drawn dumbbell (strength), figure in movement (pilates), lotus (yoga), footsteps (walk), stretch figure (mobility)
- **Utility icons**: simplified hand-drawn style, consistent stroke weight

### Cards

Oura-style: near-borderless, subtle elevation.

- Background: `#292524`
- Corner radius: 16px
- Shadow: very soft, warm-tinted, 8px blur, 2px Y offset
- No visible border
- Phase accent: 3px left-edge stripe in the phase color (workout cards)
- Padding: 16px horizontal, 14px vertical
- Expandable cards: collapsed ~80px tall, expanded full height, spring animation (Framer Motion)

### Layout principles

- Moderate density: 3-4 cards visible per screen
- 16px gaps between cards, 24px section spacing
- Phase ring: 200px diameter, centered at top of Cycle tab. The visual anchor.
- FAB: 56px circle, warm gold, bottom-right, 16px above tab bar. Subtle pulse (slow scale breath, 3% amplitude) when no workout logged today.
- Tab bar: minimal charcoal background. Custom organic icons for: Cycle, Movement, Trends, History. Active: warm gold. Inactive: stone-500.
- Transitions: spring animations (0.35s, damping 0.8). Cross-dissolve for tabs. Slide-up for sheets.

### Key visual elements

- **Phase ring**: SVG arc that fills through the cycle, color transitions through all four phase colors. Garamond hero number inside. Soft radial glow in current phase color, with visible grain behind.
- **Phase bands on charts**: semi-transparent vertical strips in phase colors behind line/bar charts
- **Workout cards**: left-edge phase stripe, hand-drawn type icon, exercise summary, volume total. Tappable to edit.
- **Calendar cells**: phase-colored fill at ~15% opacity. Workout dot indicator. Today: gold ring border.
- **Table rows**: alternating charcoal shades. Phase color dot. Tabular numbers. Tap to expand.
- **Report card header**: warm gradient of blended phase colors, heavier grain. Garamond headline.
- **Steps bar**: thin horizontal progress bar below the steps number, filling toward 8,000 in warm gold.
- **Pattern checklist**: 6 small circles, phase-colored when filled, stone-500 when hollow.

### Content tone

**Voice**: quiet, knowing friend. Direct when it matters. Never condescending, never performative.

- Suggestions: "Your body does its best lifting in follicular. Today's a good day to push."
- Deload: "Deload window. Drop volume 30%. Move for quality, not output."
- Insights: "Your squat volume peaks in follicular — that's your body's natural pattern."
- Science: "Estrogen drives muscle protein synthesis. It's high right now. Use it."
- Encouragement: "You've been in rhythm for 12 days. That matters more than any single session."
- Empty states: "Your first cycle starts here. Log your first workout when you're ready."
- Skip: no guilt. Silence. If you log a reason, it's noted, not judged.

**Phase names**: clinical. Menstrual, Follicular, Ovulatory, Luteal. The app trusts you.

### What it is NOT

- No neon, no aggressive typography, no "CRUSH YOUR GOALS"
- No gamification (no badges, no XP, no leaderboards)
- No clinical/medical UI
- No cold blues or sterile whites
- No thin hairline fonts — Garamond has weight and presence
- No literal "Indian theme park" (no Diwali colors, no om symbols, no lotus everywhere)

## User flows

### Flow 1: First launch

Open app → Onboarding (3 fields: cycle length, period length, last period start) → App calculates phase → Cycle tab with phase ring + empty state ("Your first cycle starts here.")

### Flow 2: Daily check-in (the core loop)

Open app → Cycle tab → Phase ring (where am I?) → Steps counter → Phase context card → Nutrition nudge (collapsible) → Science snippet (collapsible). To act: tap FAB to log, or go to Movement tab to see the suggestion and plan.

### Flow 3: Log strength workout

**Via FAB (primary)**: tap FAB → type picker (suggested type highlighted in gold) → tap highlighted suggestion for pre-filled log OR tap any type for blank log → search/add exercises → quick-tap sets → notes → save.

**Via Movement tab**: tap today on calendar → day sheet → tap suggestion → pre-filled log opens → adjust → save.

### Flow 4: Log session workout (pilates / yoga / mobility)

FAB → type picker → pick type → duration picker → optional notes → save.

### Flow 5: Log walk

FAB → type picker → Walk → duration → steps (auto-adds to daily total) → distance (optional) → notes → save.

### Flow 6: Log period

Movement tab "Log period start" button OR long-press a day on the calendar → pick date (today or backdate) → confirm → phases recalculate → if new cycle detected: report card generated for completed cycle, auto-backup prompted.

### Flow 7: Look up past data

History tab → segmented control (Workout History / Exercise History).

**Workout History**: lean table (Date, Phase, Type, Steps) → sort/filter → tap row to expand full detail → tap to edit.

**Exercise History**: pick exercise from search → table of every session → long-term progression review.

### Flow 8: Check trends

Trends tab → stats row (cycle day, compliance %, sessions, volume in lb) → scroll through compact cards (Rhythm Heatmap first) → tap any card to expand → change time range → collapse.

### Flow 9: Edit past workout

Movement tab day-sheet (tap past day) OR History tab → tap workout → full log reopens with all fields editable → save → all data recalculates.

### Flow 10: Phase transition

New day → app checks if phase changed → if yes: phase ring color shifts, science explainer card appears prominently on Cycle tab, suggestions adjust to new phase, nutrition nudges rotate. Explainer is dismissible, saved in Settings > Learn.

### Flow 11: End of cycle

Log new period start → app detects previous cycle completed → generates report card (compliance score, phase breakdown, comparisons, flagged patterns, positive callouts) → card appears in Trends → phase ring resets to Day 1 menstrual → auto-backup JSON export prompted.

### Flow 12: Plan future workout

Movement tab → tap a future day on the calendar → sheet shows predicted phase + "Plan workout" button → pick workout type and optionally exercises → saved as intention → when that day arrives, suggestion uses your plan instead of generating one.

### v2: Food tracking + LLM chat (planned, shapes v1 decisions)

**Food / macro tracking**: Full meal logging with macro breakdown. Tied to phase — the app knows your protein target shifts in luteal, your carb tolerance peaks in follicular, etc. Builds on the nutrition nudge cards from v1.

**LLM chat interface**: A conversational layer where you can ask context-aware questions. The LLM has access to your cycle phase, today's suggestion, your workout history, your current fatigue/skip patterns. Examples:
- "Should I do pilates today? I feel too weak for the gym." → LLM sees you're in luteal, checks your last 3 sessions, and says "Yes — your body is telling you to back off. Pilates is the right call today."
- "Should I drink my protein shake before or after my workout?" → LLM sees you're about to do heavy squats in follicular and says "After. Your MPS response is strongest post-workout in this phase."
- "What should I eat for dinner?" → LLM sees you're in menstrual, had a light yoga session, and suggests iron-rich, anti-inflammatory options with your protein target.
- "I skipped 3 days. Am I behind?" → LLM checks your cycle compliance, sees it was menstrual deload, and says "No. You were supposed to rest. You're on track."

**v1 decisions that keep v2 clean**:
- Data models are rich enough to provide full context to an LLM (phase, workout history, skip reasons, suggestions vs actual)
- Nutrition nudge cards in v1 establish the pattern that v2's food tracking extends
- The quiet-friend voice in v1 is the same voice the LLM chat uses — consistency
- Export (JSON) in v1 means the data is already structured for LLM ingestion
